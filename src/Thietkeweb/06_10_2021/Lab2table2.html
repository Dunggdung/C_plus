Apples are Good for Health
Under most condition, apple trees will set more fuit than needed for a full crop Most apple cultivars will retain this heavy set of fruit throughout the growing season resulting in small, poorly colored, low quality fruit. Thinning is the removal of a portion of the crop before it matures on the tree to increase the marketability of the remaining fruit and to reduce the biemial bearing tendency of the tree.
